# FOTOSHELL: A Biomimetic Photovoltaic Encapsulation Strategy
## Core Hypothesis and Molecular Architecture Selection (The Biochemistry Manifesto)

### 1. Abstract & Introduction (The Biochemist's Vision)
The transition to sustainable mobility requires overcoming the severe weight and aesthetic limitations of traditional, toxic, and heavy silicon-based photovoltaics. We propose **Fotoshell**, a bio-inspired, flexible Dye-Sensitized Solar Cell (DSSC) matrix designed for direct application onto vehicle exteriors as an eco-friendly "solar paint." 
Crucially, this project serves as a manifesto for Biochemistry: proving that natural, biomimetic light-harvesting molecules (porphyrins/chlorophyll analogs) can achieve the industrial durability and weather resistance of standard automotive paints when correctly encapsulated in a polymeric shell, rendering toxic heavy-metal sensitizers obsolete.

### 2. The Core Hypothesis
**Hypothesis:** By encapsulating a bio-inspired, eco-friendly Zinc-Porphyrin (ZnP) sensitizer within a transparent, highly durable polyurethane/PEDOT:PSS polymer matrix (The Shell), supported by a mesoporous TiO2 nano-scaffold, we can achieve a highly stable, room-temperature electron transfer mechanism (Artificial Photosynthesis) that resists harsh environmental degradation (UV, thermal, mechanical) while maintaining a competitive photoelectric conversion efficiency (PCE).

### 3. Molecular Selection (The "Biochemical Engine")
To avoid toxic and expensive heavy metals like Ruthenium (e.g., N719 dye) and unstable perovskites, we selected a biomimetic analog to chlorophyll:
*   **Target Molecule:** Zinc(II) Porphyrin derivative (ZnP-COOH).
*   **Role:** Eco-friendly Light Harvester (Sensitizer).
*   **Chemical Logic:** The carboxyl (-COOH) anchor groups will securely bind to the TiO2 surface. The Zinc core provides excellent excited-state lifetimes and strong absorption in the Soret (400-450 nm) and Q-bands (550-650 nm) of the solar spectrum, mirroring natural photosynthesis.

### 4. Computational Strategy (In Silico Validation)
Before wet-lab synthesis, we must prove this mechanism thermodynamically and mechanically:
1.  **QM/DFT Optimization:** Optimize the ground state (S0) geometry of ZnP-COOH to prove orbital alignment (HOMO-LUMO).
2.  **Molecular Dynamics (MD) for Durability:** Simulate the protective "Shell" (Polyurethane/PEDOT:PSS) at extreme automotive temperatures (e.g., 350K - 390K) and water solvation to prove the physical resilience of the biochemical dye against environmental degradation.
